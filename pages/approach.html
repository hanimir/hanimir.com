My approach to care

I take a collaborative, individualized approach to care. I view my role as helping patients access and strengthen the inner resources they already possess, meeting each person where they are. Psychotherapy is central to my work, with medications used thoughtfully and only when helpful or indicated.

Relational, depth-oriented psychotherapy

Therapy is a space for reflection, emotional processing, and connection. My work is informed by psychodynamic and relational approaches that explore patterns, attachment history, and the meaning we make of our experiences. We move at a pace that feels safe and supportive.

When helpful, we integrate mindfulness, somatic awareness, and skills-based tools to stabilize the nervous system and build resilience.

Shared humanity

I am drawn to treatment modalities that emphasize shared humanity, including mindfulness-based approaches and psychedelic medicine. I value offering tools that have been meaningful in my own path of growth and healing.

Medication philosophy

Conservative prescribing

We explore medications only when they align with your goals and values.

Collaborative decision-making

Every choice is transparent, with time to consider benefits, risks, and alternatives.

Whole-person lens

Sleep, stress, lifestyle, and relationships are integrated into the treatment plan.

Psychedelic integration support

I collaborate closely with therapists and provide psychiatric oversight and integration support for patients engaging in ketamine-assisted psychotherapy. Integration focuses on meaning-making, grounding, and translating insights into sustainable change.
